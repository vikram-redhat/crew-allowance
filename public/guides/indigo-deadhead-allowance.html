How IndiGo Deadhead Allowance Is Calculated (PAH §1.0)

PAH FLT Issue 01 Rev 46 · Last verified 2 May 2026 · Reading time ~6 min

IndiGo's deadhead allowance pays ₹4,000 per scheduled block hour to Captains and ₹2,000 per scheduled block hour to First Officers. It is prorated to the closest minute and applies only to DHF (deadhead-forward) sectors — those where the pilot travels as non-operating crew. The calculation uses scheduled block (STA − STD) from the airline's published schedule, not the actual block hours flown and not the Sector Value file.

The rule in one sentence

For every DHF sector in your end-of-month PCSR, the deadhead allowance is the scheduled block time in minutes multiplied by ₹4,000/60 (Captain) or ₹2,000/60 (First Officer), rounded to the closest minute and summed across the month.

The rate (effective 1 January 2026)

RankRate per scheduled block hourPer minute
Captain₹4,000₹66.67
First Officer₹2,000₹33.33

Senior Captain, LTC, TRI and DE roles use the Captain rate. Senior First Officers use the First Officer rate. Rates are taken from PAH §11.0 (the rate schedule) and are unchanged from the previous revision.

Which sectors qualify (and which don't)

Deadhead is paid only when you are positioning as non-operating crew. PAH §1.0 distinguishes two deadhead codes in the PCSR:

  • DHF — deadhead forward. You're being repositioned to operate a flight elsewhere or to return after a duty. This pays.
  • DHT — deadhead terminating. A tail-swap-style exclusion where you're listed as deadhead because of an aircraft swap mid-duty. This does not pay.

You can identify DHF sectors in your PCSR in two ways:

  1. An asterisk on the departure airport in the calendar grid on page 1 of the PCSR.
  2. The "Other Crew" section on subsequent pages — your employee number appears against the entry CP - DHF - <empno> - <NAME> for that sector.

The Other Crew section is the authoritative source. The asterisk is a shorthand; the crew details confirm it.

Edge case — DHF→DHF pairing. A duty made up entirely of two DHF sectors (an out-and-back positioning) is excluded under PAH §1.0. Both sectors must be flagged DHF in your PCSR for the exclusion to apply; a single DHF leg in an otherwise operating duty pays as normal.

Worked example

Captain A. — March, DEL base

Captain A. has three DHF sectors in their March PCSR:

  • BLR → DEL on 06 Mar — STD 14:30, STA 17:17 — scheduled block 167 minutes
  • DEL → BOM on 18 Mar — STD 09:05, STA 11:15 — scheduled block 130 minutes
  • HYD → DEL on 27 Mar — STD 22:10, STA 00:25 (next day) — scheduled block 135 minutes

Total DHF minutes for the month: 167 + 130 + 135 = 432 minutes.

432 min × ₹4,000 / 60 = 432 × 66.667 = ₹28,800

So Captain A. should see ₹28,800 on the "Dead Head Allowance-Pilot" line of their March payslip. A First Officer flying the same three sectors would see ₹14,400.

Common reasons the number on your payslip looks wrong

1. The schedule changed and the block time was different on the day

Use scheduled block as published by the airline at the time of operation, not at any later revision. If a flight was rescheduled before departure, the new STD/STA is the basis. If the flight was simply delayed on the day, that is irrelevant for deadhead — actual times don't enter the calculation.

2. The sector was reclassified from DHF to operating (or vice versa)

Crew swaps and last-minute changes can flip a sector's code. Always check the Other Crew section of your final end-of-month PCSR — that is the version used for payroll, not any earlier interim PCSR.

3. The Sector Value file was used by mistake

Sector Value (SV) is a pay metric for operating sectors. It is structurally not the same as scheduled block — SV minutes can differ from STA−STD by several minutes per sector and tens of minutes across a month. PAH §1.0 is unambiguous: deadhead is computed from scheduled block, not SV.

4. A DHF→DHF pairing was paid by mistake

A pure positioning out-and-back doesn't pay deadhead under PAH §1.0. If your DHF total includes a same-day round trip where both legs are DHF, that pair should be excluded.

Frequently asked questions

What is the deadhead allowance for IndiGo pilots?

IndiGo's deadhead allowance pays ₹4,000 per scheduled block hour to Captains and ₹2,000 per scheduled block hour to First Officers, prorated to the closest minute. It is paid only for DHF sectors — those where the pilot travels as non-operating crew to or from a duty assignment. The rate is set in PAH §11.0 and the rule is in PAH §1.0.

Which sectors qualify for deadhead allowance?

Only sectors coded DHF in your PCSR. DHF is shown by an asterisk on the departure airport in the calendar grid, and confirmed by your employee number appearing as DHF in the Other Crew section. DHT sectors do not pay, and a duty consisting of DHF on both legs (DHF→DHF) is excluded.

Is deadhead allowance based on actual or scheduled flight time?

Scheduled. PAH §1.0 specifies scheduled block (STA − STD) from the airline's published schedule. Actual takeoff and landing times are not used. Sector Value (SV) is also not used — SV is a pay metric for operating sectors and does not apply to deadhead.

How do I calculate my deadhead allowance manually?

For each DHF sector, take scheduled block in minutes (STA − STD) and multiply by ₹4,000/60 (Captain) or ₹2,000/60 (First Officer). Sum across all DHF sectors in the month. A 167-minute DHF sector pays a Captain 167 × 4000 / 60 = ₹11,133.

When is deadhead allowance paid?

PAH §1.0 states that DHF block hours for the previous month are reflected on the payslip released on or before the 3rd of the current month. End-of-month PCSR actuals are the basis.

Why does my deadhead allowance differ from the SV file total?

Sector Value is not the same as scheduled block. SV reflects operational pay for operating sectors and does not apply to DHF pay under PAH §1.0. Deadhead must be computed from STA − STD as published in the airline's schedule, not from SV minutes.

Do Senior Captains, LTC, TRI and DE pilots get a different rate?

No — Senior Captain, LTC, TRI and DE all use the Captain rate (₹4,000 per scheduled block hour). Senior First Officers use the First Officer rate (₹2,000).

What if my PCSR shows DHF but the asterisk is missing?

The Other Crew section is the authoritative source, not the asterisk. If your employee number is listed against DHF for the sector, it qualifies as deadhead even if the asterisk is missing on the calendar grid. The asterisk is shorthand only.
